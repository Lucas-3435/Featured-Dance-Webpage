The Cha-Cha Dance

The Cha-Cha is a lively, flirtatious Latin dance that originated in Cuba during the early 1950s. It evolved from the Mambo and Danzón, created by Cuban composer Enrique Jorrín. The dance is named after the rhythmic “cha-cha-cha” sound made by dancers' footwork. Known for its quick steps, hip movements, and playful energy, the Cha-Cha became popular worldwide and is now a standard style in ballroom competitions and social dancing.
